How cross-border payments actually work today

In many emerging and cross-border-heavy markets, businesses don't rely on a single financial system. A typical flow for paying an international contractor looks like:

Funds received in a local or foreign bank account
↓
Moved into a fintech platform providing USD/GBP accounts
↓
Or transferred to a crypto exchange for conversion
↓
Sent to a wallet address
↓
Sometimes off-ramped again depending on the recipient

Each step exists for a reason. But collectively, they introduce:

Delays

Due to settlement times across multiple platforms

Fragmented liquidity

Funds scattered across different systems

Operational overhead

Manual coordination between platforms

Counterparty exposure

Risk spread across multiple providers

At low volume, this is manageable. At higher volume, it becomes infrastructure debt. The current financial stack forces businesses to act as the "glue" between systems.
